### 2025-12-26 — Scene 3 Description Discipline (McDonald Principle)

**Scene Rewritten:** `content/episodes/episode-02-the-wedding-weekend/03-eddies-kitchen.md`

**Issue Identified:** Redundant description of Abby's wardrobe and Palmer kitchen in Scene 3. Both were previously established in Episode 1. Over-description violates McDonald's "lean prose" principle and authorial invisibility.

**McDonald Principle Applied:** "Established = trust reader's memory"
- Once character style or location is described, do NOT repeat in every scene
- Lean prose requires cutting all non-essential description
- Reader intelligence: they remember what you've already shown them

**Changes Made:**
- **Scene 3 opening trimmed:** Removed entire paragraph describing Abby's outfit ("soft cashmere sweater in pale gray and slim dark jeans...")
- **Scene 3 opening trimmed:** Removed entire paragraph describing Palmer kitchen ("white quartz and brushed nickel, fresh white tulips...")
- **New opening:** "Eddie was mid-sentence when Doogan walked into the kitchen. Abby stood at the stove, flipping pancakes with the kind of precision that suggested she'd rather be doing anything else." → Immediately to dialogue
- **Result:** Leaner, faster, more McDonald-consistent opening

**Element Files Updated with Anti-Repetition Guidance:**

1. **`elements/characters/abby-palmer.md`** — Added "DESCRIPTION FREQUENCY" rules:
   - ❌ DO NOT describe Abby's outfit in every scene
   - ❌ DO NOT repeat wardrobe descriptions in domestic scenes
   - ✅ Establish wardrobe once per episode (if relevant)
   - ✅ Trust reader's memory—"Abby stood at the stove" is sufficient
   - ✅ Only describe when: first episode appearance, going out, or outfit change is plot-relevant

2. **`elements/setting.md`** — Added Palmer Residence entry with anti-repetition rule:
   - Kitchen description documented (white quartz, brushed nickel, modern/curated)
   - Established: Episode 1, Scene 2; Episode 2, Scene 3
   - ❌ DO NOT re-describe in every scene
   - ✅ "Eddie's kitchen" is sufficient for returning locations
   - Rule: "Established = described once, referenced thereafter"

3. **`elements/notes.md`** — Added "Writing Discipline: McDonald Principle (Anti-Repetition)" section:
   - General rule: once established, trust reader's memory
   - Examples: Abby's style, Palmer kitchen, Doogan's Cougar, physical traits
   - When to re-describe: new episode, deliberate change, plot relevance, emotional callback
   - Why: authorial invisibility, pacing, reader intelligence, economy of prose

**Impact:**
- Scene 3 now opens faster, more dialogue-driven
- Gets to Eddie mid-sentence immediately (pure McDonald)
- Eliminates "magazine spread" feeling of over-description
- Future writers have clear guidance: describe once, then trust

**Continuity:** ✅ PASSED (description removal only; all plot/dialogue/beats intact)
