# Brenda Atwood

**Role:** Episode 3 celebrity paired with Mara for Pro-Am  
**Age:** 42  
**Occupation:** Comedic actress

---

## Physical Description

**Height:** 5'6"  
**Build:** Average, curvy - not athletic but not out of shape  
**Hair:** Auburn/reddish brown, shoulder length, usually styled casually  
**Eyes:** Brown  
**Skin:** Fair, freckles across nose and cheeks  
**Distinguishing Features:** Expressive face, great smile, laugh lines around eyes

**Style:**
- On course: Borrowed golf attire that doesn't quite fit right. Practical but not fashionable. Hat that shades face but messes up hair. Looks slightly uncomfortable in golf clothes.
- Off course: Casual chic - comfortable but stylish. Lots of scarves, boots, jeans.
- Natural makeup approach
- Reading glasses that she keeps losing

**Vehicle:** Probably Volvo or Subaru - safe, reliable, mom-car energy even though she's not a mom

---

## Background

### Career
- **Known For:** Former SNL cast member (5 years, beloved by fans)
- **Current Work:** Successful sitcom actress (plays a lovable, neurotic character)
- **Style:** Self-deprecating humor, physical comedy, great timing
- **Fame Level:** Recognizable, respected, not A-list but solidly successful
- **Awards:** Multiple Emmy nominations, won once for SNL writing

### Golf Experience
- **Skill Level:** Bad. Genuinely bad.
- Took lessons specifically for this Pro-Am (6 weeks of weekly lessons)
- Can hit the ball but direction and distance are unpredictable
- Understands rules and etiquette but struggles with execution
- Her practice rounds were "disasters but fun ones"

### Why She's Doing the Pro-Am
- **The Cause:** Genuinely passionate about women's empowerment and STEM education
- Daughter is 10 and loves science - this is personal
- Show runner suggested it (good publicity)
- Thought it would be funny material for talk show appearances
- Wanted to challenge herself with something she's terrible at

---

## Personality

**Core Traits:**
- **Self-Deprecating:** Makes fun of herself constantly, especially her terrible golf
- **Warm and Genuine:** No pretense, no ego
- **Smart:** Sharp wit, quick with observations
- **Game:** Willing to look foolish for a good cause or a good joke
- **Gracious:** Thanks Mara constantly for patience

**Sense of Humor:**
- Observational comedy - finds absurdity in situations
- Physical comedy - not afraid to look ridiculous
- Conversational - makes people feel like they're in on the joke
- Never mean-spirited - laughs with people, not at them

**With Mara:**
- Genuinely in awe of Mara's skill
- Apologizes profusely for terrible shots
- Makes jokes to ease tension when she screws up
- Asks good questions about golf strategy
- Respects Mara's focus - knows when to be quiet
- Provides unexpected moments of comic relief

---

## Golf "Skills"

**What She Can Do:**
- Hit the ball (sometimes)
- Follow basic etiquette
- Not hold up play too much (she plays fast because she knows she's bad)

**What She Can't Do:**
- Hit straight
- Judge distance
- Read greens
- Chip consistently
- Drive more than 150 yards

**Her Approach:**
- Treats each shot as an adventure
- Laughs at her own failures
- Cheers loudly when she makes contact
- Makes jokes about her form
- Keeps spirits up

**Her Pro-Am Partner:**
- Brings her bumbling, very dramatic male assistant to be her partner
- He's even worse at golf than she is
- Over-the-top reactions to every shot ("Oh my GOD, Brenda, did you SEE that?!")
- Makes everything into a theatrical production
- Provides constant comic relief throughout the week
- Makes Brenda look competent by comparison
- His terrible golf becomes running joke

**Famous Moment:**
- During practice round, hit ball into water, said "At least the fish will have something to talk about"
- Immediately went viral on social media
- She's leaning into being "that celebrity who's terrible at golf but having fun"

---

## Character Function in Episode 3

**Purpose:**
- **Contrast to Candy:** Shows you can be fun and funny without being annoying
- **Comedy Relief:** Provides light moments during tense tournament
- **Mara's Warm-Up:** Mara has to be patient with Brenda, which softens her before Doogan arrives
- **Genuine Connection:** Not fake Hollywood - real person who connects with Mara as human to human

**Dynamic with Mara:**
- Brenda's self-awareness is endearing
- She never pretends to be better than she is
- Her humor isn't at anyone's expense
- Mara finds herself smiling despite herself
- Brenda notices when Mara loosens up: "Was that a smile? I just saw a smile!"

**Dynamic with Doogan:**
- They'd have great banter
- Both have sharp wit
- He respects that she's not trying to BS anyone
- She might clock that Doogan is interested in Mara and gently encourage it

**Comic Beats:**
- Terrible drives that go sideways
- Cheerful acceptance of bad shots
- Self-narrating her failures
- Genuine joy when she makes par (even with handicap strokes)
- Comparing golf to comedy: "In comedy, you bomb and people laugh. In golf, you bomb and people wince."

---

## Relationship with Tilly and Candy

**Observational Target:**
- Finds them fascinating in an anthropological way
- Makes mental notes for future material
- Not mean about it, just bemused
- Might say to Mara: "They're like if Instagram became sentient"
- Her comedy about them would be gentle mockery, not cruelty

## Why She's Not Annoying (Unlike Tilly/Candy)

1. **Self-Aware:** Knows she's bad, doesn't pretend otherwise
2. **Respectful:** Treats golf as a real sport, not a backdrop
3. **Quiet When Needed:** Knows when to shut up during others' shots
4. **Quick:** Plays fast because she doesn't want to hold anyone up
5. **Genuine:** Not performing for cameras - just being herself
6. **Funny:** Her humor comes from intelligence, not desperate attention-seeking

---

## Notes for Writing

- Use her as palate cleanser between serious Mara moments and annoying Candy moments
- Her bad golf should be endearing, not frustrating
- Show Mara warming to her despite herself
- Brenda can be the first crack in Mara's armor - she makes Mara smile before Doogan even arrives
- Don't overuse her - she's seasoning, not main ingredient
- Her comedy works best when understated

- Makes it entertaining and genuine
- Shows how to be funny without making it all about yourself (unlike Candy)
