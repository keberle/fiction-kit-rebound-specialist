## Relative Time

---

## Summary
Sebastian LeClere (using the alias Albert Stans) operates Theta Health Insurance while simultaneously running a medical billing company and strip-mall clinic. The scheme targets terminally ill patients for life insurance fraud: Theta issues life insurance policies to dying patients, then uses fake prescriptions and medical billing to hide the patients' true conditions from life insurance carriers. Investors buy into the scheme expecting insurance payouts when patients die, but Sebastian double-scams them by inflating medical costs and billing them for fake treatments, ensuring the real profit goes to him while investors lose money covering endless "medical expenses."

---

## The Core Scheme: Life Insurance Fraud

### Basic Mechanism
1. **Target Selection:** Terminally ill patients (cancers, heart disease, late-stage illnesses)
2. **Insurance Policies:** Theta Health issues life insurance policies to dying patients
3. **Hidden Illness:** Medical records altered, prescriptions issued under fake names
4. **Life Insurance Payout:** When patient dies, life insurance carrier pays out (unaware of pre-existing terminal condition)
5. **Investor Scam:** Investors think they're buying into life insurance fraud, but get double-scammed via inflated medical costs

### Why It Works (Initially)
- Life insurance carriers don't pay for pre-existing terminal conditions
- If carriers knew patients were dying, policies would be denied/voided
- Medical privacy laws make cross-checking difficult
- Small strip-mall clinic below regulatory radar
- Pharmacy run by elderly couple (unknowing participants)

## The Medical Components

### Strip-Mall Clinic
- **Location:** Non-descript strip mall (below regulatory radar)
- **Staff:** Doctor (later vanishes), Nurse Nancy, temp receptionist
- **Purpose:** Treats terminal patients, issues fraudulent prescriptions
- **Patient Selection:** All dying, all covered by Theta Health insurance
- **Annabelle's Role:** Receptionist who flagged suspicious patient files

### Prescription Fraud Pattern
- **Issue:** Prescriptions written in one name (e.g., "Patricia Weller")
- **Pick-up:** Different person picks up medication (actual patient)
- **Purpose:** Hide real patient's terminal condition from life insurance carriers
- **Coverage:** All prescriptions covered by Theta Health
- **Red Flag:** Ellie (pharmacy tech) notices pattern—same name repeatedly, different people picking up

### Pharmacy: Unknowing Participants
- **Owners:** Elderly couple, no clue about fraud
- **Location:** Used for "creative prescriptions" (Nancy's slip)
- **Staff:** Ellie (granddaughter), notices pattern but doesn't understand significance
- **Episode 5:** Baxter's first date with Ellie reveals prescription anomaly
- **Episode 8:** Ellie gives team access to pharmacy files

### Medical Billing Scam (Double-Cross)
- **Sebastian's medical billing company** bills investors for patient treatments
- **Inflated costs:** Medications and procedures far exceed actual expenses
- **Investor trap:** "Downside is you have no idea how much the investment will cost"
- **Result:** Investors cover endless medical bills while Sebastian pockets difference
- **Episode 9 Discovery:** Eddie at sports bar learns from investor about unlimited costs

## The Investor Scam

### How Investors Are Recruited
- **Pitch:** Buy into life insurance policies on terminal patients
- **Promise:** When patients die, life insurance pays out (big return)
- **Hook:** Unethical but profitable—appeals to greedy investors

### The Hidden Costs
- **Medical Bills:** Investors must cover all medical expenses for patients
- **Why:** If life insurance carriers see unpaid medical bills, they investigate (might discover terminal condition)
- **Problem:** Medical costs are unlimited and escalating
- **Sebastian's Double-Cross:** He controls medical billing, inflates every charge
- **Investor Trap:** They're locked in—can't pull out without losing everything already spent

### Episode 9 Sports Bar Revelation
- Eddie playing pool with cagey investor
- Eddie asks about "downside" of investment
- **Investor:** "Downside is you have no idea how much the damn investment will cost"
- Eddie confused: "Insurance premiums are set, right?"
- **Investor:** "You have to cover medications and other medical bills so life insurance doesn't figure out how sick they are"
- Bills are "not small"

### Why Investors Get Scammed
- **Sunk cost fallacy:** Already invested money covering medical bills
- **No exit:** Pulling out means losing everything
- **Sebastian controls billing:** They can't verify charges
- **Pride:** Admitting they're victims means admitting they tried to commit fraud

## Witness Elimination Strategy

### Why Annabelle Was Killed
- **Role:** Receptionist at strip-mall clinic (real employer)
- **Danger:** Flagged suspicious patient files in clinic database
- **Knowledge:** Saw pattern of terminal patients with mismatched prescriptions
- **Threat Level:** High—could expose prescription fraud to authorities
- **Solution:** Murder disguised as serial killer (frame Doogan)

### Why Nancy Was Killed
- **Role:** Nurse at strip-mall clinic, administered prescriptions
- **Danger:** Revealed pharmacy connection to Doogan ("creative prescriptions")
- **Knowledge:** Knew which pharmacy issued fake prescriptions, probably knew why
- **Lifestyle:** Expensive (plastic surgery, cash payments)—being paid well to keep quiet
- **Threat Level:** High after team investigation started, Nancy might talk
- **Solution:** Second murder reinforces Doogan frame-up narrative

### Why Doctor Vanished
- **Role:** Ran strip-mall clinic, prescribed fraudulent medications
- **Danger:** Questioned by police multiple times
- **Knowledge:** Knew entire scheme—patients, prescriptions, insurance fraud
- **Threat Level:** Critical—police pressure might make him turn
- **Solution:** Disappeared (fled? killed? unclear in outline)
- **Episode 8:** Martinez reveals doctor vanished

## Resolution (Episode 10)

### Arrests
- **Sebastian LeClere/Albert Stans/"John":** Conspiracy to murder, insurance fraud, medical billing fraud
- **Curtis (wine forger):** Manufacturing drugged wine, accessory to murder
- **Investor (sports bar guy):** Insurance fraud, attempted murder (pulled gun)
- **Unnamed killer:** Murder charges (two counts)

### Charges Dropped for Doogan
- **DA (Claire Rogers):** Circumstantial evidence, time suck case
- Nexapan in blood proves Doogan couldn't have committed murders
- No defensive wounds on victims (unconscious when killed)
- Wine source (fake winery) proves elaborate frame-up

### Final Outcomes
- Team cleared of murder accusations
- Solicitation/fraud charges dropped (Claire's call)
- Sebastian's scheme exposed, life insurance carriers notified
- Investors lose money (fraud victims themselves)
- Team warned: "Stay off the radar" (one free pass from Claire)
