# The Drugged Wine Murders

## Relative Time
**Episode 1 (Day 1, 17:00)** — First murder (Annabelle Anders)
**Episode 4** — Second murder (Nurse Nancy)

---

## Summary
Sebastian LeClere (using multiple identities) orchestrates two murders using fake-sealed wine bottles containing Nexapan, an oral sedative. The wine is manufactured by Curtis, a paroled wine label forger, who creates realistic counterfeit bottles using cheap boxed wine blended with sedatives. Both victims are killed after they and Doogan consume the drugged wine, ensuring Doogan is unconscious and unable to defend himself or the victims. The murders serve to eliminate witnesses to the Theta Health insurance fraud scheme while framing Doogan for the crimes.

---

## The Murder Weapon: Woodmark Vineyards Wine

### Manufacturing Process
- **Wine Source:** Cheap boxed wine (low quality, easily sourced)
- **Drug:** Nexapan (oral sedative) precisely blended so it doesn't settle
- **Packaging:** Mass-purchased wine bottles with fake "Woodmark Vineyards, Napa" labels
- **Sealing:** Professional fake seals that appear factory-authentic
- **Distribution:** Given to victims as "gifts from a client"—victims trust sealed bottles

### Why Nexapan
- Oral sedative (works when consumed in liquid)
- Takes 20-30 minutes to take effect (allows normal dinner conversation)
- Effects escalate gradually: Pleasant buzz → lightheaded → disoriented → loss of coordination → blackout
- Victim and frame-up target both incapacitated
- Traceable in blood work (but police need to know to test for it specifically)

### Wine Label Forger: Curtis
- Paroled years before story start
- Previous conviction: High-end wine label forgery scam (featured in documentary)
- Sebastian's partner in current operation
- Mentally deficient, morally empty (takes pride in "special batches")
- Regular business: Date-rape wine (Rohypnol-infused bottles for "sleazy guys")
- Special Nexapan batch: Commissioned by Sebastian for specific targets

## The Frame-Up Strategy

### Why Frame Doogan
- Team hired to investigate Annabelle after first rebound job
- Doogan's business (rebound specialist) makes him appear predatory
- Pattern of seducing women makes frame believable
- Two murders with identical MO point to serial predator
- Police focus on Doogan instead of insurance fraud

### Frame-Up Mechanics
1. **Wine delivery:** Sealed bottle appears legitimate (victim trusts it)
2. **Timeline coordination:** Sebastian needs to know when Doogan will be with victim (Eddie's status calls provide this)
3. **Drug dosage:** Both victim and frame-up target incapacitated
4. **Doogan left alive:** Dead suspect can't be prosecuted, frame only works if he's present
5. **Staged scene:** Doogan in bed with victim, appears to be intimate partner
6. **Plausible narrative:** Rebound specialist's job makes predatory behavior believable

### Flaws in Frame-Up (Eventually Exposed)
- **Drug in Doogan's system:** Can't murder someone while unconscious
- **No defensive wounds:** Victim unconscious when killed
- **Wine source:** Woodmark Vineyards doesn't exist (no real winery, can't purchase anywhere)
- **Pattern too perfect:** Two identical murders with same MO too convenient
- **Sebastian's connections:** Multiple identities eventually link back to wine forger

## The Wine Scam Business Model

### Regular Operation (Date-Rape Wine)
- Target market: "Sleazy guys" who want to drug dates
- Product: Sealed wine bottles with Rohypnol
- Advantage: Fully sealed bottle looks legitimate (no suspicious drugging of glass)
- Distribution: Underground network (Curtis's connections from previous scam)

### Special Nexapan Batch
- Custom order from Sebastian
- More precise blending required (Nexapan doesn't settle like Rohypnol)
- Curtis "really proud" of technical achievement
- Limited production (only for murder targets)
- Higher price point (specialized service)

## Additional Notes

### Why Frame-Up Eventually Fails
1. **Drug evidence:** Can't convict unconscious person of murder
2. **Wine source:** Non-existent winery raises questions
3. **Pattern too neat:** Two identical murders suggest professional hit
4. **Team's investigation:** Exposes insurance fraud motive
5. **Martinez's surveillance:** Builds case against real killers
6. **Claire's judgment:** DA recognizes circumstantial evidence insufficient

### Legal Resolution
- DA drops murder charges (circumstantial, time suck)
- Sebastian arrested for insurance fraud, conspiracy to murder
- Curtis arrested for manufacturing drugged wine, accessory to murder
- Investor arrested for insurance fraud
- Doogan gets "one get-out-of-jail-free card" from Claire
- Team warned: Stay off radar
